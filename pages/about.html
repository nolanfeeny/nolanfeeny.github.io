About

Nolan Feeny grew up in Okemos, MI and has lived there his whole life until moving to Ann Arbor for school in the fall of 2016. In his free time, Nolan enjoys being outside in nature, playing sports, and immersing himself in music. He grew up camping, playing hockey, studying cello and piano, and now produces his own music in his free time.

Nolan is a rising Junior at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor. He is majoring in Industrial & Operations Engineering (IOE), with an International Minor for Engineers. He plans to complete a masters in IOE as part of the Engineering Global Leadership Honors Program (EGL) at U of M. Outside of classes, he is a consultant with MECC Consulting Group, plays cello for the Campus Symphony Orchestra, and enjoys football and hockey seasons. Before graduating from Okemos High School in 2016, he won multiple state-wide music awards and featured in the OHS Earth Club's first 'Green Tip of the Week'.

In the future Nolan hopes to attain positions in both consulting and automotive industries. Overall he wants to use his combined engineering, computer, and business skills to reduce inefficiencies and optimize the amount of good in the world - through improving sustainability and social practices on a global scale.
